01 — The Problem

650 million Indians are uninsured — not for lack of demand

How can AI improve financial inclusion by helping underserved, underinsured individuals understand, choose, and act on term insurance — through personalised, trustworthy, simple guidance, without requiring financial knowledge or complex infrastructure? That was the brief I set myself after advising retail clients on term insurance at HDFC and watching the same objections repeat: confusion, distrust, and a product that assumed a level of financial literacy most buyers don't have.

Four structural barriers

Financial literacy & misconceptions
Premium is widely misunderstood as a "loss" rather than a necessary protective shield.
Language barrier
Guidance is English-first, but 80%+ of the target users speak Hindi or a regional language.
Digital literacy friction
Existing apps need 10+ steps and PDFs; this audience already lives on WhatsApp voice notes.
Trust deficits
Jargon-heavy policy wording and a history of mis-selling create a structural wall of mistrust.

I validated this against secondary research rather than assumption — app store reviews on Policybazaar, and threads on r/IndiaInvestments and r/personalfinanceindia surfaced a consistent pattern: agents pushing savings policies instead of term cover, unclear exclusions, and claim processes nobody could explain in plain language.

02 — Research

Voice of the customer

Five structured interviews using the Jobs-to-be-Done framework, on top of the secondary research, surfaced four themes that shaped every downstream decision:

  • Trust deficit — past agent fraud means radical transparency has to be the baseline, not a feature. The bot must never ask for cash or Aadhaar upfront.
  • Awareness gap — many users already hold a PMJJBY cover but don't know it, and can't explain their own policy terms or claim process.
  • Nominee blindspot — women are routinely excluded from insurance decisions and left stranded at claim time with no guidance.
  • B2B2C demand — insurance agents spend 30+ minutes per client on basics and want a tool that guides clients, not one that replaces them.

03 — Design

From wireframe to shipped product

Low-fidelity wireframe

Before writing a line of backend code, I mapped the full 8-step conversation as an interactive HTML wireframe — welcome → language → intent → learn/buy branch → recommendation → agent handoff — to pressure-test WhatsApp's List Message constraints (title ≤24 characters, description ≤72) before they became a build-time surprise.

Shipped product

The same flow, live on WhatsApp, in Marathi — language confirmation, the Learn path's dynamic topic menu, and a Buy-path outcome showing three ranked plans with CSR ratios after the bot collects income and expense data conversationally.

04 — Architecture

A dual-call agentic pipeline

A WhatsApp message hits a Meta webhook, which lands on a FastAPI server backed by a session store. The server fires two concurrent Claude Haiku calls — one explains the concept in-language, the other generates the next set of dynamic follow-up options — before either touches the hardcoded 8-plan-plus-CSR dataset that ultimately hands off to a licensed agent.

Five engineering decisions that define the product

  • Model choice: Claude Haiku — roughly 10× better multilingual instruction-following than Groq/Llama in testing.
  • Dual-call design: one AI call explains; a separate, concurrent call generates dynamic follow-up options, so the user is never staring at a static menu.
  • Triple-lock language enforcement: system prompt + per-message injection + code-level drift detection, so a Hindi conversation can't quietly slip into English mid-thread.
  • Temperature 0.3: prevents hallucination; every plan premium and CSR ratio is hardcoded, so Claude cannot invent a number.
  • ≈₹0.50 per session: conversation history capped at 8 messages, on the lowest-cost Anthropic model — the unit economics have to work at 50,000 MAU.
05 — Business Model

Free to the user, funded by distribution partners

The end user pays nothing. Insurers, NBFCs, and agent networks pay a flat SaaS fee plus a referral fee per completed policy — deliberately flat rather than value-based, so the product has zero incentive to push more expensive plans over the right one.

06 — Compliance & Ethics

Guidance only, by design

The bot never handles payment or policy issuance — a licensed agent always completes the transaction. No Aadhaar or biometric data is collected by the bot itself, and the product is built to be compliant with India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act from day one, not retrofitted later.

  • No hallucinations: all 8 plan premiums and CSR ratios are hardcoded in the system prompt; temperature 0.3 keeps responses factual and consistent, with a weekly red-team audit before each release.
  • Nominee protection: a mandatory education module collects nominee details upfront — 4 of 5 interview families had this exact gap.
  • Radical inclusion: PMJJBY (₹36/month) is auto-recommended for users declaring income under ₹8,000, and the bot runs on WhatsApp itself — no new app to install.
